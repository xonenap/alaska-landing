Grizzly Bear

North American subspecies of brown bear, are powerful, omnivorous mammals known for their large shoulder hump and "grizzled" fur. They are solitary, opportunistic omnivores, eating plants, berries, insects, and animals, and hibernate in winter, giving birth to cubs, usually twins, in their dens. Found in western North America.

Alaskan Moose

are the world's largest deer, massive herbivores found throughout Alaska, known for their imposing size (up to 1,600 lbs) and iconic status, living solitary lives in boreal forests, eating twigs and aquatic plants, and sometimes interacting with humans in towns, fiercely protective of calves, and formidable against predators like wolves and bears despite deep snow challenges.

Bald Eagle

is North America's iconic bird of prey, famous for its white head/tail and brown body, serving as the U.S. national symbol, named not for baldness but from an Old English word meaning "white".

Mighty Glaciers

Alaska is home to over 100,000 glaciers, covering 5% of the state. These massive ice rivers have shaped the landscape for thousands of years.

From Glacier Bay to Mendenhall, each ice giant tells a unique story of our planet's history.

Fast Fact:

The Hubbard Glacier is North America's largest tidewater glacier, and unlike many others, it is currently advancing!
